## ACT I: ARRIVAL & INNOCENCE

### Chapter 1: Arrival

**Timeline**: 1965 frame → 1952 past
**Artifact Trigger**: Faded postcard ("The elephants look tired but the band sounds fine...")
**Word Target**: 6,000–7,000

**Beats**:
1. 1965: Tommy opens storage box, finds postcard
2. Flashback triggered—train arrives, Tommy steps into circus world
3. Sensory overload: smells, sounds, chaos of setup
4. First glimpse of Jenny on practice trapeze (distant, luminous)
5. Meets Rafael (brief, professional—rigging check)
6. Meets Bobby Kellerman (instant connection, both outsiders)
7. Assigned to band; meets bandleader Vic
8. Opening night performance—Tommy's nerves, near-miss
9. Post-show: sees Jenny and Rafael together (first jealousy seed)
10. Closing: Tommy writes sanitized letter home

**Ends on**: Wonder mixed with unease—something beneath the surface

### Chapter 4: Red Lights

**Timeline**: 1952 (mid-season)
**Artifact Trigger**: Torn ticket stub from the night everything changed
**Word Target**: 6,000–7,000

**Beats**:

## ACT II: COMPLICITY & SILENCE

### Chapter 5: Unequal Portions

**Timeline**: 1952 (mid-season continues)
**Artifact Trigger**: Grease-stained handbill (cookhouse schedule)
**Word Target**: 6,000–7,000

**Beats**:
1. 1965 frame: handbill brings smell of cooking back
2. Cookhouse scene—clear disparity in what workers vs. performers eat
3. Clara's complicated position—she serves everyone, sees everything
4. Michael Torres introduced—mechanic, U.S. citizen, "papers always ready"
5. Michael's warning: "Silence is survival, kid"
6. Tommy and Jenny's relationship develops—stolen moments
7. Rafael notices; tension builds in triangle
8. Birmingham headlines in newspaper—wider world intruding
9. Gus Kellerman pressures workers harder (money troubles)
10. Closing: Tommy knows too much to stay innocent, not enough to act

**Ends on**: Complicity deepening; pressure building

## ACT III: RECKONING & TESTIMONY

### Chapter 9: The Weight

**Timeline**: 1952–53 (off-season) → Texas
**Artifact Trigger**: Letter from mother praising his "adventure"
**Word Target**: 6,000–7,000

**Beats**:
1. 1965 frame: mother's handwriting triggers memory
2. Tommy returns to San Antonio
3. Family celebration—they're proud of his circus summer
4. Tommy can't tell them anything real
5. Silence as habit—lying by omission becomes natural
6. College begins; Tommy performs normalcy
7. News from circus: official story is "accident"
8. Jenny's bitter message: don't contact me
9. Rafael's death reduced to paperwork
10. Closing: Silence isn't absence—it's weight

**Ends on**: Living with complicity; silence as prison

### Chapter 11: The Mirror

**Timeline**: August 20, 1965 (entirely present)
**Artifact Trigger**: Newspaper—"Seminary Student Slain Shielding Young Woman"
**Word Target**: 6,000–7,000

**Beats**:
1. Morning routine—Tommy at 30, comfortable, hollow
2. Opens newspaper; headline about Hayneville
3. Reads about Jonathan Daniels—pulled Ruby Sales down, took blast
4. **(TWIST #4)**: Unbearable parallel—Daniels did what Tommy couldn't
5. Tommy's hands shake; can't continue reading
6. Walks through day in dissociative haze

8. Daniels: 26, seminary student, chose to stay in danger
9. Tommy: saw shotgun, froze, let Rafael die
10. Closing: "Why did he move and I didn't?"

**Ends on**: The question that breaks open 13 years of silence

### Chapter 12: Testimony

**Timeline**: 1965 (days after Daniels' death)
**Artifact Trigger**: All artifacts spread on table
**Word Target**: 6,000–7,000

**Beats**:
1. Tommy retrieves storage box
2. Spreads everything on table: postcards, letters, programs, clippings, journal pages
3. Each artifact is a chapter of silence
4. Considers: who would he tell? Why now?
5. Jenny's accusation echoes: "You're not a witness—you're an accomplice"
6. Daniels' example: testimony through action
7. Tommy's choice: testimony through words
8. Begins to write: "In the summer of 1952, I joined the circus..."
9. Not redemption—reckoning; the dead stay dead
10. Closing: The cost of breaking silence is everything; the cost of keeping it was more

**Ends on**: First words of testimony—novel circles back to its own beginning
